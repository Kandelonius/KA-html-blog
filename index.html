First post

Posted on: June 13th 2019

I first became interested in computer science because I wanted to be involved in making games. I have had an interest in games my whole life, and I wanted to be part of the industy that has given me so much joy over the years. I knew that in order to get into computer science I'd have to do a lot of math, which I actively avoided in high school.
When I decided to go back to school I had to start at pre algebra because I had been out of school so long and forgotten what little I once knew. I had a long road ahead of me but I had decided it was time to get out of my entry level employment loop and try to invest myself into something that interests me. I noticed as I worked my way through the mathematics I actually started to like it quite a bit. I have always enjoyed puzzles and various forms of problem solving. I am glad I was able to get past my initial distaste for math because I really do enjoy the world of STEM it has allowed me to enter.

Second post

Posted on: June 14th 2019

One thing I found while working through my math was Khan Academy. I kept getting stuck on my problems and I would look up the individual concepts and this same person with really clear explanations kept popping up. I eventually dug deeper and found that there was a website with all of these videos in one place. After I found KA I started to find other open source websites. I was amazed at how much was available for free. By the time I got to my first programming class I was using a site to follow the Stanford equivilant of the java class I was taking at my local community college.

Lambda post

Posted on: March 6th 2020

My 'why' for Lambda school project day 3. I have had a long history with post high school education but have not been able to make it work for myself. I have had advocates in the past but I kept getting hung up on something. Fresh out of high school I lacked motivation and any direction, so I just entered the job market doing whatever I could for a job. After 2010 I found the motivation I needed and returned to school for computer science and was able to do really well as long as I focused and kept my goals in mind. Once I got into university that changed though because I was no longer able to make things work financially due to the fact that it requires more money to go to university than it does a community college. I enjoy the problem solving aspects of programming and the step by step nature of the problem solving process, so I believe this is the correct path for me. I really like that Lambda School gives people the opportunities that are lacking in other areas of society regardless of background. I hope that I will be able to pay back the full $30k for my tuition because that will mean I am making more money than I ever expected I would be making. I also sincerely hope I work for a company I am proud of and I believe if I am in an industry where I am likely to earn a livable wage will have the luxury of being picky in that way. Thank you for giving me this opportunity, I will try my best to meet or exceed what is asked of me.
